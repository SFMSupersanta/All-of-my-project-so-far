Income Statement and Balance Sheet




As your fifth assignment this semester, you are to upgrade your balance sheet program to accomodate an income statement and transaction and account descriptions. 


Learning Outcome

Upon successful completion of this assignment, you will have demonstrated the ability to use one-dimensional array data structures, including strings.


Problem Analysis

For an introduction to bookkeeping programs and balance sheet accounts, see the background section of assignment 4.

A financial report for a company includes not only a balance sheet, but also an income statement.  The income statement summarizes the amounts earned and the amounts spent over the accounting period since the most recent balance.  The difference between the amounts earned for the period and the amounts spent to earn that income is the gross profit for that accounting period.

The accounts that track income are called "revenue" accounts.  Examples of revenue accounts are service fees billed and sales made.

The accounts that track spending are called "expense" accounts.  Examples of expense accounts are rent, salaries and supplies.

Changes to revenue or expense accounts are recorded through transactions.  For example, recording an office supplies' purchase involves two transactions: one crediting the bank account, and the other debiting the office supplies account (which is an expense account).  Recording a sale involves two transactions: one debiting the bank account, and the other crediting the sales account (which is a revenue account). 

Crediting a revenue account increases its (credit) balance, while debiting a revenue account decreases its (credit) balance.  Conversely, debiting an expense account increases its (debit) balance, while crediting an expense decreases its (debit) balance.  The relationships of transaction debits and credits to increases and decreases in account balances for the different account types are: 


                           ---------Transaction---------
 Account       Balance        Credit              Debit
 -------------------------------------------------------
 Asset         Debit         decrease           increase
 Expense       Debit         decrease           increase
 Liability     Credit        increase           decrease
 Revenue       Credit        increase           decrease
 Equity        Credit        increase           decrease 


Specifications

Your income statement and balance sheet program can

  • process transaction entries to revenue and expense accounts,
  • display an income statement,
  • accept and display transaction descriptions, and
  • accept and display account titles
Account numbers from 4000 to 4999 identify revenue accounts.  Account number from 5000 to 5999 identify expense accounts.

Your program should display a menu that offers the user five options:

  • enter a transaction
  • view the general journal
  • view the balance sheet
  • view the income statement <== NEW
  • quit the program
If the user chooses to enter a transaction, your program prompts the user for
  • an account number (between 1000 and 5999)
  • a brief description of the transaction (30 characters maximum) <== NEW
  • whether the transaction is a debit or a credit
  • the amount of the transaction (always a positive number)
If the user has entered the account number for the first time, your program
  • creates the new account and
  • prompts the user to enter a brief (also 30 characters maximum) title for that account.  <== NEW
Once the user has entered the transaction amount, your program returns to the menu.

If the user chooses to view the general journal, then your program displays all transactions that have been entered, in the order entered and includes their descriptions.  The format of the general journal matches this sample:

    2000 Future Shop                                  2100.99

    2000 Staples                                       569.90

If the user chooses to display the income statement, your program displays the balances for all revenue and expense accounts for which transactions have been entered.  Your program calculates each account balance by starting at zero and accumulating all of the transaction amounts for that account.  The format for the income statement matches the following sample: 

                       Income Statement
 Account Description                         Debit     Credit 
 ------- ------------------------------ ---------- ----------
    4000 Professional Services                        6000.00
                                        ---------- ----------
             Total Revenues                           6000.00

    5100 Rent                              3000.00
    5400 Office Supplies                    569.90
                                        ---------- ----------
             Total Expenses                3569.90

                                        ---------- ----------
             Net Income                               2430.10

Note how subtotals are shown for the revenue and expenses account types and the net income is the difference between total revenues and total expenses.

To close the company books for an accounting period the user enters transactions that bring the revenue and expense account balances to 0.00 and adds the net income to the retained earnings account: 

 Account Description                         Debit     Credit 
 ------- ------------------------------ ---------- ----------
    4000 Close Professional Services       6000.00
    5100 Close Rent                                   3000.00
    5400 Close Office Supplies                         569.90
    3200 Net Income                                   2430.10

If the user chooses to display the balance sheet, your program displays the balances for all asset, liability and equity accounts along with the account descriptions.  The format for the balance sheet matches the following sample:

                                        ---------- ----------
             Total Liabilities                        4069.90

    3000 Common Shares Outstanding                    4000.00
    3200 Retained Earnings                            2430.10
